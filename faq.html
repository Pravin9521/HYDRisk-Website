Frequently Asked Questions

Here you'll find answers to common questions about flood risk, our services, and how HYDRisk can help you.

General Questions

Flood risk assessment is the process of identifying and evaluating the potential for flooding at a specific location and understanding its likely impact. It's crucial for protecting lives, property, and investments, as well as for meeting regulatory requirements for new developments or existing structures in flood-prone areas.

Anyone planning a new development (residential, commercial, industrial), making significant changes to an existing property, or those concerned about flood exposure, especially in areas identified as flood zones, should consider a flood risk assessment. It's often a mandatory requirement for planning permissions.

Climate change is increasing the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, leading to higher rainfall, rising sea levels, and more unpredictable flooding. This means that historical flood data alone may not be sufficient, and future flood risks need to be carefully assessed and modelled.

Questions about Flood Risk Assessments (FRA)

We assess various sources of flooding, including:

  • Fluvial Flooding: From rivers and watercourses overflowing their banks
  • Pluvial Flooding: Surface water flooding due to heavy rainfall overwhelming drainage systems
  • Groundwater Flooding: When the water table rises above ground level
  • Reservoir/Dam Break Flooding: Inundation from the failure of a dam or reservoir
  • Coastal Flooding: From sea level rise, storm surges, or tidal influences

The timeframe for an FRA depends on the complexity and size of the site, the available data, and the specific requirements of the project. We aim to provide timely and efficient services, and we'll provide a detailed timeline in our proposal.

Generally, we'll need site location details, proposed development plans, any existing hydrological reports, and information about historical flooding if known. We'll guide you through the specific data requirements during our initial consultation.

Questions about Hydraulic Modelling

Hydrological modelling focuses on the overall water cycle, predicting rainfall-runoff processes and water flow volumes in a catchment. Hydraulic modelling then takes these outputs to simulate how that water moves through specific channels, floodplains, and infrastructure, predicting water depths, velocities, and flood extents. They often work hand-in-hand for comprehensive analysis.

We utilize industry-standard, advanced hydraulic modelling software (e.g., HEC-RAS, InfoWorks ICM, MIKE FLOOD) to ensure accuracy and reliability in our simulations. Our team is proficient in the latest tools and techniques.

Yes, definitely. By simulating various flood scenarios, hydraulic modelling provides crucial information for developing effective emergency response plans, including identifying evacuation routes, critical infrastructure at risk, and areas that require immediate attention during a flood event.

Questions about Drainage Strategies

SuDS are a collection of water management practices that aim to mimic natural drainage processes. They focus on managing surface water runoff close to its source, often through methods like permeable pavements, rain gardens, swales, and detention ponds, reducing flood risk and improving water quality.

A well-designed drainage strategy is vital for preventing surface water flooding, protecting your property from water damage, complying with local regulations, and often contributing to the overall sustainability of your development by managing stormwater effectively and reducing environmental impact.

Yes, we specialize in designing drainage strategies for both new developments and existing properties. We can assess your current situation and propose sustainable solutions that are appropriate for your site and needs.

Questions about Levelling and Grading

Proper levelling and grading create a stable and even foundation for your structures, which is critical for their longevity and safety. More importantly, it directs rainwater away from buildings, preventing water accumulation, erosion, and potential structural damage due to water ingress.

By shaping the land to create appropriate slopes, levelling and grading ensure that surface water runoff is efficiently directed away from critical areas and towards designated drainage systems. This prevents localized flooding and reduces the overall flood vulnerability of the site.

HYDRisk provides the expert design, analysis, and planning for levelling and grading. While we don't perform the physical earthwork ourselves, we provide detailed plans and specifications that can be implemented by your chosen construction contractors. We can also provide oversight and consultation during the implementation phase.

Questions about Climate Risk Flood Analysis

A standard Flood Risk Assessment (FRA) typically evaluates current flood risks based on historical data and existing conditions. Climate Risk Flood Analysis, on the other hand, specifically incorporates future climate change projections (e.g., IPCC scenarios) to predict how flood risks might evolve over various time horizons (e.g., 2050, 2080). It's about future-proofing your assets against a changing climate.

We utilize projections from reputable climate models and authoritative bodies such as the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), national meteorological services, and regional climate agencies. This ensures our analysis is based on the most up-to-date scientific understanding of climate change impacts.

While not always mandatory, it is becoming increasingly critical for long-term investments, large-scale infrastructure, and projects in vulnerable areas. Many investors, insurers, and even some regulatory bodies are now requesting or requiring climate risk assessments as part of due diligence. It's a proactive measure for true long-term resilience.
