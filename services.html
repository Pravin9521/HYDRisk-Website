Flood Risk Assessments (FRA)

Detailed studies to understand flood potential and consequences for new and existing developments. Essential for safety, planning, and insurance.
What it is: A Flood Risk Assessment (FRA) is a detailed study to understand the potential for flooding at a specific location and the likely consequences. It's crucial for any development, whether it's a new build or an existing structure, especially in areas prone to flooding. We consider various sources of flooding, including rivers, heavy rainfall, groundwater, and even potential dam failures.
  • Safety: Protects lives and property by identifying vulnerable areas.
  • Planning Permission: Often a mandatory requirement for securing development approvals in flood-prone zones.
  • Insurance: Helps in obtaining appropriate flood insurance and understanding true risk exposure.
  • Design & Mitigation: Informs the design of your project to incorporate flood-resilient measures, reducing potential damage and operational downtime.

Hydraulic Modelling

Simulate water behavior in rivers, drainage systems, and floodplains to predict flow, depth, and velocity for better planning and safety.
Hydraulic modelling uses sophisticated computer software to simulate how water behaves in a given environment. We create digital twins of rivers, drainage systems, and floodplains to predict water flow, depth, and velocity under different flood scenarios. This allows us to visualize the potential impact of flooding before it happens.
  • 1D/2D hydraulic modelling (HEC-RAS, MIKE, InfoWorks)
  • Flow path simulations
  • Rainfall intensity and duration analysis
  • Catchment area studies
  • Culvert, Bridge, mitigation simulation

Climate Risk Flood Analysis

Assess future climate change impacts on flood risk, including sea-level rise, storm intensity, and long-term adaptation strategies.
We assess how future climate change scenarios will impact your flood risk, helping you build resilience for the long term. This includes climate scenario modeling (RCP 2.6, 4.5, 8.5, etc.), sea-level rise impact assessments, projected changes in storm intensity and runoff, long-term flood risk mapping, and strategic recommendations for climate adaptation.
  • Climate scenario modeling (RCP 2.6, 4.5, 8.5, etc.)
  • Sea-level rise impact assessments (coastal zones)
  • Projected changes in storm intensity and runoff
  • Long-term flood risk mapping
  • Strategic recommendations for climate adaptation
